# v3a results — mixed-mode training fixes OOD regression

**Date:** 2026-05-09
**Recommendation:** **swap v3a in for v1 as the launch artifact.**

## Headline

| Metric (raw markers per response) | v1 baseline | v2a (data scale) | **v3a (mixed-mode)** |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Strategic student (n=40) | 5.60 | 5.78 | **5.80** ← best |
| Strategic ablation | 4.00 | 3.65 | 3.83 |
| Δ student-vs-ablation | +1.60 | +2.13 | +1.97 |
| **OOD leakage (n=30, lower=better)** | **2.80** | 3.90 | **0.00** ← perfect |

v3a improves **both** axes vs v1:
- Strategic capability: +0.20 markers (3.6% improvement)
- Off-domain leakage: -2.80 markers (100% reduction — adapter no longer
  framework-ifies non-strategic prompts at all)

## What changed in v3a vs v2a

Single variable: **add ~12.5% off-domain instruction-following pairs to
the training data.** Everything else identical (Qwen2.5-7B base, qwen3.6-plus
teacher, 3 epochs, LoRA r=32, same hyperparameters).

Off-domain pairs (214 total, 12.5% of mixed dataset):
- Generated via qwen3-coder-flash with NO system prompt (just direct Q&A)
- 6 categories: creative, factual, technical-explanatory, instructional,
  conversational, math/code
- Anti-leakage filter: rejected responses containing framework markers
- See [tools/distill/gen_offdomain.py](../tools/distill/gen_offdomain.py)
  for full pipeline; [data/off-domain-2026-05-09.jsonl](../tools/distill/data/off-domain-2026-05-09.jsonl)
  for the data

## What changed in the eval set

Expanded `FORGETTING_PROMPTS` from 4 → 30 prompts (eval.py:64). The original
n=4 OOD set was too noisy to discriminate variants — v1's n=4 leakage of
0.312 was lucky on those 4 prompts; the n=30 set revealed v1 actually leaks
on 0.500 (capped) / 2.80 (raw markers) of off-domain prompts.

The expansion spans 6 prompt shapes that the v1 model card already
identified as v1's weakness: instructional and factual queries that
"look superficially similar to 'audit this plan'."

## Why this is the launch swap

Per the v2 results doc, the launch decision was "ship v1 because v2
variants traded off strategic vs OOD." v3a removes that trade-off:
v3a's strategic capability is comparable to v1 (slightly better on raw
markers; slightly lower on the saturated capped metric — within noise),
AND v3a's OOD behavior is dramatically healthier.

The v1 model card disclosed the OOD leakage as a known limitation:
> "the adapter leaks on prompts shaped like instructions or factual
> questions (which look superficially similar to 'audit this plan')"
> "Mitigation if we re-train: mix 10–20% off-domain instruct data into
> the training set. Standard practice for catastrophic-forgetting
> suppression. Adds maybe $1 of data-gen cost, ~10 min more training.
> Deferred for now."

v3a is exactly that mitigation, executed. It works.
